Type 1 Diabetes

Type 1 diabetes is thought to be caused by an autoimmune reaction, where the body attacks itself by mistake, stopping the production of insulin. It's usually diagnosed in children, teens, and young adults. If you have type 1 diabetes, you'll need to take insulin every day to survive. Currently, no one knows how to prevent type 1 diabetes.

Type 2 Diabetes

With type 2 diabetes, your body doesn't use insulin well and can't keep blood sugar at normal levels. It's usually diagnosed in adults (but more and more in children, teens, and young adults). You may not notice any symptoms, so it's important to get your blood sugar tested if you're at risk. Type 2 diabetes can be prevented or delayed with healthy lifestyle changes, such as losing weight, eating healthy food, and being active.

Gestational Diabetes

Gestational diabetes is hyperglycaemia with blood glucose values above normal but below those diagnostic of diabetes. It occurs during pregnancy, affecting some women who previously didn't have diabetes. Women with gestational diabetes are at an increased risk of complications during pregnancy and delivery. Additionally, both the mothers and possibly their children are at increased risk of developing type 2 diabetes in the future.

Understanding Geriatric People

Geriatric people, often referred to as senior citizens or the elderly, are individuals who have reached an advanced age. This stage of life typically begins at the age of 65 or older. Aging is a natural process that brings about various physical, mental, and emotional changes.

As people age, they may face challenges related to mobility, chronic health conditions, memory decline, and social isolation. However, it's crucial to recognize that each person's aging journey is unique, and many seniors maintain active and fulfilling lives.

Caring for geriatric individuals requires compassion, understanding, and tailored support. Providing access to quality healthcare, social interactions, and mental stimulation can significantly enhance their well-being and overall quality of life.

Let us cherish and respect the wisdom and experiences of our elderly population, appreciating the invaluable contributions they have made throughout their lives.

Diabetics Around the World

Last updated 3 mins ago

Diabetes is a global health challenge affecting millions of people around the world. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), the prevalence of diabetes has been steadily increasing in both developed and developing countries.

Efforts to combat diabetes include raising awareness about risk factors, promoting healthier lifestyles, and improving access to diabetes care and treatment. Regular screenings, proper management of blood sugar levels, and adherence to prescribed medications are crucial in preventing complications associated with diabetes.

Through international collaboration and community education, we can work towards reducing the burden of diabetes and improving the quality of life for diabetics worldwide.

The Future of Diabetics

Diabetes is a chronic condition that affects millions of people worldwide. As we look to the future, advancements in technology and medical research hold the promise of transforming the lives of those living with diabetes.
